# Game Tester Questions
1. How did the controls feel? Were movement and shooting intuitive or confusing?
2. How did the power up system feel to you? Did the randomness feel exciting, frustrating, or balanced?
3. Did the difficulty feel fair? Were enemy waves too easy, too hard, or inconsistent?

Tester 2 Feedback
1. Controls were pretty easy to understand, and movement felt good.
2. I liked the randomness of the power ups but a couple of them felt noticeably stronger than others which made certain moments less cool.
3. I thought the difficulty curve was okay but waves could use more variety. After a few rounds it felt like I had already seen the same same.

Tester 3 Feedback
- The controls were great, it was really easy to get adjusted to the and were intuitive. Felt similar to the controls for other shooter games.
- The power up system was exciting and well balanced for the most part. It was interesting to roll a number and get a new power up. The system was one of the things that made me want to keep playing in order to explore other power ups.
- The difficulty was quite fair, especially at the beginning. However, it would be nice to increase the overall difficulty of the game a bit more by incorporating more difficult enemies rather than a steady increase of “triangle” enemies.
- Yes, the game’s visuals were quite clear especially with the power up effects. There were some times that the power ups would appear a bit off screen though.
- The parts that were the most fun was picking up a power up and hearing the different sound effects that would be triggered, as well as passing a round.
- I experienced some bugs in the tutorial as I got stuck during it, but the main mechanics, style, and audio of the game was quite polished. I’d focus more on either polishing the graphics of the main game rather than just having shapes and adding more difficulty with the enemies.
- I can definitely see how additional things can be added on top of this game (more power ups, dice, enemies, levels, art, etc.) that would make it fun and keep players to want to keep playing the game.

# Summary Comparison

All three testers liked the controls of the game, they all agreed that the movement and the shooting made sense intuitively. One of the testers was of elementary school age and was able to pick it up quickly. So the core player input is solid. The power up system was a highlight for some players but they noticed some were more overpowered than others. All playtester noted that more clarity is needed to tell what power up is activated and what it does. Maybe change the number system to images was a suggestion. Testers wished for much more variety in enemies and enemy spawning. More interesting patterns of enemies can make the game feel fresher in later rounds. Tutorial bug was found by one tester, the other testers did not play the tutorial. Visuals need to be completed still and audio needs to be dialed in much more. Overall, my game has a strong start, but it's got a lot of issues in specific areas that could be fixed to make the player experience a much more positive one.
